A look into the IDM

Radiohead's album “Kid A” is often hailed as a groundbreaking work that significantly influenced the IDM (Intelligent Dance Music) genre. Released in 2000, the album marked a departure from the band's earlier rock sound, embracing electronic music and experimental elements.

Tracks like “Everything in Its Right Place” and “Idioteque” showcase the band's innovative use of synthesizers, drum machines, and unconventional song structures. The album's atmospheric and often eerie soundscapes drew inspiration from artists like Aphex Twin and Autechre, blending ambient, electronic, and jazz influences.

“Kid A” challenged listener's expectations and pushed the boundaries of what popular music could be, making it a seminal work in the IDM genre. Its impact is still felt today, as it continues to inspire musicians and producers exploring the intersection of electronic and alternative music
